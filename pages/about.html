About Us

When it comes to African vegetables, there’s a rich variety of leafy greens and other nutritious options.

Let’s explore some common African vegetable dishes: Spinach Stew (Efo Riro): A popular West African dish, Efo Riro features spinach stirred with spices, condiments, and a pepper sauce. It’s often prepared with meat, crayfish, and other flavorful ingredients1. Collard Greens (Sukuma Wiki): Sukuma Wiki, widely consumed in East Africa, consists of tender collard greens. These greens are cut, cooked, and sometimes fried in oil. They’re typically served alongside cereal grains or tubers2. Jute Leaves Soup (Ewedu Soup): Ewedu soup, made from jute leaves, is a staple in Nigerian cuisine. The leaves are cooked to create a slimy, nutritious soup that pairs well with pounded yam or other starchy sides1. Waterleaf Soup: Waterleaf, a succulent green, is used to prepare a flavorful soup. It’s a common ingredient in Nigerian and Cameroonian cooking, often combined with meats and spices1. Pumpkin Leaves Stew (Ugu): Ugu stew features tender pumpkin leaves. These leaves are cooked with various seasonings and proteins, resulting in a delicious and wholesome dish1. Bitter Leaf Soup (Ofe Onugbu): Bitter leaf soup is a specialty in Igbo cuisine (Nigeria). The bitter leaves are cooked into a hearty soup, often accompanied by fufu or pounded yam1.
